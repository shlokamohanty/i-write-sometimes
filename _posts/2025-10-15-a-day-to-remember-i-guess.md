Today has been a fun but intense day! I woke up really excited. I love fall, and I love layering. Then, suddenly, on the subway, I didn’t know why. Out of the blue, I decided to play Self-Love by Metro-Boomin and Coi Leray. Then I started thinking that it’s been a long time since I’ve watched Spider-Man across the Spider-Verse. So, the new game plan was to watch Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse and work on animation on the side. I still remember the summer of 2023 when the movie was released. I was hooked to my seat. Two weeks ago, I was asked if there was an artwork that moved me, and I didn’t have an answer. But I think I do have an answer, and funny enough, it is quite literally this movie. Many of my mixed media animations have been largely inspired by the style used in Spiderman. The track, along with everything else about the film, makes it what it is. Surprisingly, people can have such well-known pieces of work that truly inspire them. Then there’s mine, which is this specific Spider-Man movie. The fact that there’s a character, Hobie, animated in 4 FPS, and LEGO animation made by a kid in the same film is unbelievable.


So, my game plan for today was to do something different since I didn’t have work that required much cognitive attention, like cropping frames from my prints while watching Spiderman. It was so good. I had my headphones on full volume and didn’t pay attention to anyone around me. Until my computer crashed, and everything that I had worked on for about an hour and a half wasn’t saved. Well, this was more of a storage issue. So I started deleting documents and rearranging things. Then I came across a video I had edited two summers back. It was a short movie that my old friend group from college and I made. I basically directed the movie/ edited it/ and handled the music. The actors improvised the dialogue, and we all came up with the story together. We decided to make the movie because we were struggling with our own issues. One of my roommates had made living in my own space a hellish experience by that point, and two others were going through a heartbreak. One friend really missed her true calling, which was fashion design. It was a pretty rough semester for everyone in that group. I was doing a credit overload at this point, and I dropped from 20 to 18 because it was a difficult semester (still a lot, I know).


The group breaks apart after this semester, so we never finish the movie.


![image (4)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1877485f-87d6-43db-959e-a2dfb219ddb3)


Anyway, so we come up with the most bizarre Rick and Morty-ish, omni-loop plot line, where a scientist who is so extremely brilliant at her work, but her brilliance made her push people in her life, except for one friend, who is struggling with severe depression, travels through different universes to find a timeline where she’s happy. But in every universe, she sabotages all her relationships, and the only one that stays is where she can find happiness: her relationship with her best friend. Well, anyway, I finally uploaded the unfinished shitty movie as an unlisted video on YouTube so I can finally delete it. It’s so wild that I wouldn't be here if I weren’t editing this video a summer back to relearn my love for making movies, because that is the summer when I was confused about whether I wanted to do a master's in economics and data science after graduating. But then I thought about it more thoroughly, and I found the courage to tell my parents that this is genuinely my interest at the intersection of arts and sciences, specifically in the realm of filming. I created numerous portfolio projects, dropped my statistics major, and instead focused on economics and data science, while also taking up digital media art classes. This movie was so bad, but it honestly got me to where I am now, because if it weren’t for making this shitty movie, I wouldn’t be pursuing this.


Three summers and so much has changed, and I’m happy that I’m where I am now, creating things every day (some I’m more into than others, but it’s worth it).


![image (5)](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2213ee39-0c54-406d-8714-a70d0fcd05d0)


By 8:30 pm, I was feeling a bit burnt out, so I headed home, and my roommates and I decided to paint. I show them this shitty movie, and they are like I had too much free time in college to do this. It takes me a while to open up, but something about painting, talking, and my day in general made me feel safe enough to share a lot today. We might not be Jean-Michel Basquiat/Claude Monet/Henri Matisse. But it was a fun day. At 9:00 pm, I didn’t want to go to school on Wednesday, but now I'm excited again to go to school.

---
Unfortunately, today, I realized that Spider-Man doesn’t really move me emotionally, like an artwork should when you talk about an artwork moving you. It moves me more technically, but it is still one of my top movies.
