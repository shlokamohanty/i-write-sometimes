<div class="day-block">
<span class="day-label">day 1</span>
I feel like I’ve become a research experiment, or a lab rat, for myself, which is funny, but also interesting. I decided to start by illustrating something related to lunch at school today, and Jatin. I remembered this, and while thinking about it, I also started thinking about when Sonal was mad at me. Then I realised, at least at first, that whenever I deliberately try to find a memory, the involuntary ones might just come up. So I changed one part of my process: ‘If, while looking for a memory, an involuntary one surfaces, I’ll sketch the voluntary one first and the involuntary one the next day’. Sketching this was fun, but I realised I have to come up with more alias names, except for certain people whose names I can actually use.
</div>

<div class="day-block">
<span class="day-label">day 2</span>
This memory now feels really silly. I didn’t feel anything much while sketching, even though it creeped up on me yesterday while I was thinking. But one thing about sketching on paper is that it’s hard to make the same people look alike. Digitally, it’s easy. I could have traced it or used ‘onion skins’, but it’s harder to do that on paper. Perhaps I need tracing paper. It’s also 2:27 am. I need to start putting this on my calendar.
</div>

I have to wake up by 9 at the latest, but I fixed the edits, hehe. I’m a little obsessive, perhaps, or is this what they call rigour? I need sleep. I love GitHub pages.

<div class="day-block">
<span class="day-label">day 4</span>
I was feeling low in general today, because my group’s idea in one of my classes wasn’t understood the way I wanted it to be, and I was feeling tired, and just one of those days, I couldn’t help but compare myself to others in the cohort. Then learning that NYU doesn’t even have an E-Verify program was a cherry on top, but it’s okay. I went home, found my roommate in even more distress, tried to make her laugh for a bit, then started illustrating really late, because I had a bunch of chores to finish, but that actually made me feel a whole lot better, so I made two. They were cute memories. But I do want to push myself. I know the moment I start thinking of performing or music-related memories, it’ll get into that painful zone of illustrations. I wonder how many involuntary thoughts I’ll have, but there are only so many? Anyway, I was talking to Amisha this morning, and they nudged me to remember a memory I had forgotten. Then I tried to remember, and realised, I’ll have to add an addendum, which is “If someone prompts me to remember a memory, but I had previously forgotten, and after the prompt, I voluntarily search for it, I will categorize it as a ‘low activity’ memory.” and also added a trigger warning, for other possible memory sketches, for when and if they come along. Also, just why do I have so many memories associated with Subway? I didn’t even realise that it was such a prominent part of my teenage experience.
