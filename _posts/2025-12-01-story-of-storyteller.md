I often think that I’m just a sad story.

I’m not much of a comic, but maybe if I were, I’d have much more to offer. I could probably have sold my story for laughs. Perhaps that would have been easier.

Because when I tell my story otherwise, I see their eyes and see their pity. If things were that bad, why couldn't I tell when they turned bad? I guess I was only 13, I guess I was only 17, I guess I was only 22.

So I don't tell my story. Hold my breath as long as I can, but eventually the story starts to slip.

You know the pieces now and connect the dots. You’re the detective in this mystery novel now.

The mystery is interesting. It’s complex. Almost nearing the end, and at the end of this labyrinth, it’s just a sad story.

But I often wonder, why do people love to read sad stories? Catharsis, maybe? To teach you more about yourself. I suppose?

I learnt something today. That while recalling your own tragedy, you tie the sadness and the anxiety together. But while reading a tragedy, you can experience the sadness without the anxiety.

So, the reader of this sad story. Maybe this story will just pass you by.

But I’ll try to make this stay with you. I’m a storyteller, as you already know. I’ll build out the plot, the characters, and the setting. I’ll tell you the sad story, just the way you want to hear. The story of a storyteller.

So, what do you choose, a mystery or a sad novel?
