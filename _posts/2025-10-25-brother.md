I recently realized that most people have vivid memories of their lives before the age of 10. Unfortunately, I don’t remember much about my life before age 10 or my childhood. Maybe it’s just my poor memory. Anyway, when I was 9 and 10, I remember everything really vividly. I do remember a little bit of 7, too, actually, it’ll be a lie if I say I don’t. My mom had me when she was 24, and I was the first grandchild, the first kid of the house. My aunt and my mom have about a 2-year age gap. She got pregnant with my cousin when I was 7.

My aunt used to live in Australia, but when she got pregnant with my cousin, she took maternity leave and moved to India for that year and a half. She moved because there was this one time, because of her nausea, she had to go to the ER in Australia, and they put her in triage for four hours, gave her a medicine, then waited for her to puke. Still, she didn’t, so they sent her back home, and then the moment she went home, she puked and then had to return to the hospital. The healthcare system in Australia was shocking to her, and with very little support around her through the initial bit of her pregnancy, she decided to give birth in India.

My aunt recently told me that I used to watch so much Shin-Chan and Doraemon after coming back from school and before going to school, and that she had such bad nausea in the first three months of pregnancy that even to this day, if she hears the words Shin-Chan and Doraemon, she’s conditioned to feel like throwing up.

Anyway, I also vividly remember my aunt’s morning sickness, then she gets scared of dengue in Thane because it’s monsoon season, so she moves in with my grandma in Rourkela. Nirvaan was born in IGH Rourkela in November. During winter break, when I went to Rourkela, I met Nirvaan for the first time. It’s one of my first times holding a baby. My aunt and uncle were also very careful with Nirvaan because he was a newborn baby, and they had thoroughly read ‘What to Expect When You’re Expecting.’ You’re not supposed to touch a baby without hand sanitizer—no cheek kisses. You’re supposed to hold the head of the baby at a specific position. Babies don’t sleep on pillows. They even had a sanitizing station used to keep clean bottles in. So I learned a lot about what needs to be done when a baby is born. (babies can be a lot). During this time, my aunt, even though she was so happy, was so stressed. This was my first time learning what post-pregnancy looks like.

While this was going on, a friend of mine suddenly left during the same winter break. I still remember this even though I was 7. His parents got divorced that year, and his mom got custody of him, and they moved to Bangalore. She didn’t reach out to my mom until years later. We used to be really close, to the extent that my mom was like, ‘he’s like a sibling to me.’ We used to be really close. He and I once broke a window at a restaurant.

After Nirvaan was born and my best friend at the time had just left, my mom realized she might still be able to have another kid, thanks to my incessant begging for another sibling. Because everyone in my class had siblings, she thought that was the right decision. So, ‘team baby’ was my mom, my dad, and me. I appreciate that my mom wanted to be really direct with me, but I don’t think a 9-year-old needs to know the details of pregnancy, not to mention to the extent that I did. I went to all the gynaec appointments with my mom, so every day I reminded her to take her calcium and folic acid pills while she was trying to get pregnant.

When you’re 35, it’s considered that the pregnancy is high risk, and my mom didn’t want to announce the pregnancy until the anomaly scan, which is in the second trimester, just in case she tried to abort. So the first four months of the pregnancy I had to keep this a secret from everyone, my friends, and also my grandparents, who were actually staying with us for a month at that point.

I had only lied once before that, in 2nd grade. My Hindi teacher used to be really strict, and I was really scared of her, so one time, I pretended to be sick and went to the infirmary. I knew lying was really bad before that, so I always felt really guilty about lying to everyone until I was, well, 9. Even when other aunties would probe me with questions about my mom’s belly showing, I would deflect. After the 4 months, though, when my parents announced to my grandparents, I was really excited, and then I told all my friends. My parents and I had three girl names picked out, Zara, Saanchi, and Sia, and we settled on Siddhant as the boy’s name. My pick was Sia, to keep the ‘SM’ initials. My mom’s pregnancy, unlike my aunt’s, was really smooth. The funniest memory from that pregnancy was when my mom, my dad, and I traveled across Europe. My dad and I would make songs about AJI-NO-MOTO, an MSG product, because when you are pregnant, you are supposed to avoid it. The whole trip, we would look into the ingredients of food packets to make sure they didn’t contain that. My mom is a runner, so when she was 6 months pregnant, she ran a 10 km marathon and came third, which is really impressive. I remember the day she got contractions. In the morning, she went to the gym and still ran on the treadmill. My family is really athletic, by the way, I’m the black sheep, with a concerningly low fitness level, to be honest.

My brother was born on 18 August, 4:55 pm, I remember that so well because that day I was at home, and everyone was in the hospital, then at 4:30 pm, my grandpa gets a call saying my mom is in labor, then he goes to shower, and prays, and as we are headed out, we get a call saying he’s born.

So I was already clear with the drill from my aunt’s pregnancy, and was very careful with everyone, making sure they put on enough hand sanitizer before pulling my brother’s cheeks. My brother essentially grew up with three parents. That was the ‘team baby’. The moment he was born, I knew that I would leave the house, and he’d only be 8, so I used to be really sad about that because I barely remember my life before 8, and I thought about what was going to happen to him. But he remembers a lot more, or at least he hears so many stories that he can relate more.

When he was just a few months old, he wouldn’t eat unless I performed for him, but that became exhausting really quickly, so I got him onto YouTube. He became an “iPad kid” very early on and would only eat if something was playing in the background. That’s when I began watching nursery rhymes with him. So I know an unhealthy amount of nursery rhymes, than a normal person should, and I also think this is what really got me into animation.

There was a day I remember when he wouldn't sleep, so I sang him lullabies. He fell asleep on my lap, and I felt so proud.

I’ve done everything for him that a parent does for a baby. Changed diapers, cleaned up throw-up, and all the other nasty things you need to do, even though they feel nasty, because you care and you love.

I know, it’s a very early preview of parenthood.

The older he grew up, and while I was in my first two years of undergrad, I saw a lot of similarities in him that I did with the guys I grew up around, because he likes football, really into it, and also, you know, how teenagers who like sports are, I guess. I quickly realized that it is just me projecting. So I decided to work on that a lot.

He’s 12, and I’m 22. Much older. Now, both of us are much more mature. But, we both get into a big fight, which helps, because we’ve both had this pent up emotions, he thinks I don’t like him because he plays football, that I’m not his friend and that he wants to be my friend, and the truth is he’s right, I messed up, I’ve always treated him like I’m his parent. Also, really need to work on not projecting onto him, so I began learning more about football from him and understanding and changing my perspective on it. He’s a brilliant, creative, and sensitive kid, and I’m sure he’s going to do really great things. We both talk much more now about things in our lives. Not just superficial things. And I miss him every day and wish he lived much closer. So finally, I exit the ‘team baby’, after 12 years, and now my brother and I are even closer, and onto a new team together.
