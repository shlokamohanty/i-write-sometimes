I’m not the most observant person. I’m the most unobservant person that I know.

There’s a pier by downtown Brooklyn near the Dumbo, I’ve been there 5 times, but each time I think it’s a different spot. Only recently, when I went there, did I realize I had been there so many more times.

I cannot even tell when a man has shaved his face or not, or if someone has had a haircut.

Last December, my roommate had a breakup with her boyfriend, and I didn’t even realize that she was rattled the night of her breakup. She is a chatty one, but that day she just went up to her room. She later told me she had a breakup that day. I didn’t really notice that, though.

I often think about things that miss by me, because I’m unobservant.

What if I hadn’t noticed the cherry tree mosaic on the Manhattan-bound side of the F train in Delancey and Essex? Or the fish mosaic on the Brooklyn-bound side of the F train in Delancey and Essex.

Or that it’s much easier to jump the turnstile from the Havemeyer St entrance into the Marcy Av station.

There’s a sticker on the Broadway entrance into Marcy Avenue that reads 'Keep kids on leash.'

I think about the 6-foot pandemic distance markers on Jay Street Metrotech. Each of them is stained. But the stains are patterned. 2020 doesn’t seem like a distant past. Should I make an animation of the distance markers? I swear there is a similar dot stain in all of the distance markers.

Every Tuesday, and possibly other days as well, churros and fruit salads are sold on the Brooklyn/Jamaica Center-bound side of the J/M line at Delancey and Essex.

It’s also one of the few stations I know with a bodega within the station.

On weekends, there’s always a performance on the Manhattan-bound side of Delancey and Essex. Why are so many people going to Brooklyn on the weekend? Are they going to get off on York St to go to the Brooklyn Flea?

Whenever I hold on to the subway poles, I immediately think of the scene from past lives. Is there really _inyun_?

I look at couples leaning on each other. A mom supporting her kid on a scooter while the kid is dressed as Spiderman, friends going somewhere together, filled with love.

There’s so much graffiti on the Williamsburg Bridge.

I think about the 82 in Madison. I used to take it every night from Waters to Witte. The bus stopped by Regent every night because that’s where the bus driver swapped. Who knew I'd stay by the Regent for three years after that?

Indie coffee used to seem so far away that year.

I think about when I used to take the bus from Saxony at 11:55 p.m. every night. University Avenue looked so different from the way it did when I moved to Madison.

Whenever I passed by Chamberlin Hall, I thought of my second year in undergrad. I'd stay up all night and sleep during the day. I barely saw the sun for four months. It wasn’t the silence that got to me.

Am I the most unobservant person I know, or do I choose to be?

Maybe I want to let things miss by me.
