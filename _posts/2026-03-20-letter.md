When I turned 19, someone I was really close to from 6th grade, but with whom I eventually stopped talking, sent me an email in the form of a letter written for me when I turned 18. He had given me a letter on another one of my birthdays, too, but, from what I heard, it was oddly similar to one he wrote for somebody else, so I vowed then never to write a letter to anyone and despised them. So, I didn’t open the email until one day last year, when I was 23. That email was a bittersweet ride down memory lane, but it didn’t make me feel seen at all. It made me realise that he saw me very differently from who I am. So I decided to take on a project, with letters as a medium, as an attempt to make people feel seen.


What started as writing letters for a project became a means for me to process and express my feelings and practice more vulnerability, as I’ve always found it difficult to communicate those through spoken words, and these letters became that bridge. So I wrote these for people around me, for some estranged relationships where I left quietly, and some more abstractly about experiences that I’ve had with people. I wrote around 59 letters over four months, all in disguise, before I stopped, as they started becoming my primary form of expressive communication, and that very reason started hindering my relationships. Now, I’m still learning to communicate verbally in a vulnerable and articulate way, but this was a step towards that and perhaps getting over the childish fear of letters in general. This article is a space for me, and perhaps for those I’ve written to, for accessing those letters, time and time again. A trigger warning, as some of the letters mention elements of sexual assault and violence.
